Pizza is a world-famous Italian dish consisting of a flattened, leavened wheat dough base topped with tomatoes, cheese, and various other ingredients, which is then baked at a high temperature.

Traditionally cooked in a wood-fired oven, it is celebrated for its incredible versatility and cultural significance. In fact, the art of traditional Neapolitan pizza-making is recognized as a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage.

📜 Historical Facts

  • Ancient beginnings: Long before the modern version, ancient Greeks, Egyptians, and Romans ate flatbreads topped with olive oil and local herbs.

  • The word is ancient: The term "pizza" was first documented in 997 AD in a Latin manuscript from Gaeta, Italy. It stated a tenant had to give 12 pizzas to the local bishop every Christmas and Easter.

  • Born in Naples: Modern pizza as we know it emerged in Naples, Italy, during the 18th century as a cheap, portable street food for the working poor.

  • Royal approval: In 1889, the classic Margherita pizza was created to honor Queen Margherita of Savoy. The ingredients matched the colors of the Italian flag: red tomatoes, white mozzarella, and green basil.

  • WWII boom: Pizza exploded in popularity in the United States after World War II, when American soldiers returned home craving the dish they had discovered while serving in Italy.

😀 Fun & Quirky Facts

  • The technical term for crust: The raised outer edge of a pizza crust is officially called the cornicione.

  • Canadian invention: Despite its tropical name, the popular Hawaiian pizza (ham and pineapple) was actually invented in Canada in 1962.

  • Massive consumption: In the United States alone, an estimated 350 slices of pizza are eaten every single second.

Homemade or Restaurant Pizza? 🤔

Homemade pizza is ideal for customization, budget-friendly cooking, and total control over ingredients and calorie counts. On the other hand, restaurant pizza offers an unmatched, authentic taste and fluffy texture due to professional high-temperature ovens that home kitchens cannot duplicate.
